Pulmonary Hypertension

A disease that affects the heart and lungs

Pulmonary Hypertension
is a form of high blood pressure that affects the heart and lungs. It usually gets worse over time. Pulmonary hypertension affects arteries in the lungs and the right side of the heart.

Symptoms
  • Chest pains,
  • Dizziness,
  • Fatigue,
  • Inability to exercise,
  • Low blood pressure,
  • Chest pressure,
  • Chronic cough,
  • Fast heart rate,
  • Shortness of breath,
  • Swelling,
  • Structural damage to the lungs,
  • Light headedness,

Diagnosis
  • Use catheter to measure the blood pressure in the left atrium,
  • Do an Echocardiogram (ultrasound probe of heart from different angles),
  • Xray lungs,
  • Analyze gas levels in blood,
  • Pulmonary functions test,

Treatments
  • Antihistamines or inhailers (if caused by Asthma),
  • Anticlogent,
  • Diuretic (increases urine production),
  • Vasodilator (widens blood vessels),
  • Oxygen therapy,
  • Physical aerobic excersize,
  • Surgery to remove clots,
  • Lung transplant,
