Pulmonary Hypertension

A disease that affects the heart and lungs

Pulmonary Hypertension
is a form of high blood pressure that affects the heart and lungs. It usually gets worse over time. Pulmonary hypertension affects arteries in the lungs and the right side of the heart.

Symptoms include
  • Chest pains,
  • Dizziness,
  • Fatigue,
  • Inability to exercise,
  • Low blood pressure,
  • Chest pressure,
  • Chronic cough,
  • Fast heart rate,
  • Shortness of breath,
  • Swelling,
  • Structural damage to the lungs,
  • Light headedness,
